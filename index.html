Why save something on the blockchain?

It's forever

As long as the Ethereum blockchain exists, your message does as well. And with 25K nodes + a bright future ahead, it's unlikely the whole blockchain will disappear any time soon.

Datetime stamp

Your message is saved in a block of the blockchain, which has a date and time. That certifies when you wrote and saved the message, and no one can argue that.

No tampering

A block in the blockchain cannot be modified (otherwise the blockchain would become invalid from thereafter), and so is for your message. Once it's saved, it can't be modified.

No censorship

Since a block can't be modified, it also can't be deleted, which means your message can't be censored. Is there any centralised website that can guarantee you that?

What can you do with msgblocks?

Write and store a love letter for your partner, or a date-certified prediction of the future, or...

Password protection

Save your text in clear, or protect it with a password so that only you, or the people with your password, can retrieve it later.

Optional comments

Your message can be a statement that doesn't allow replies or comments...or not! If it's a love letter, let your lover reply on the blockchain!

Printable certificate

Print a certificate with your message's transaction hash, its block number and the timestamp. This will make a great gift for a geek ;)

User interface for humans

You can use any block explorer to retrieve your messages, but msgblocks has a nice, easy interface for it. Especially if you got replies.
